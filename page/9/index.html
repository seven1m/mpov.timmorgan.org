Junk Email Filter Dot Com

Jun 24, 2006
We just started using an email filtering service at Cedar Ridge. So far, I’m very pleased with it. In the two days it’s been in place, only a handful of spam messages have passed through to our server, and every one of those were filtered by our existing DNS blacklist filtering. The filter works by proxy, meaning all mail destined for our domain is first delivered to junkemailfilter.com. Their server filters out the spam using some fancy voodoo and delivers the good stuff to our server.

Less Spam

Jun 21, 2006
I have an ongoing experiment in which I never empty my Spam folder in Gmail. On the left side of the screen, when I log in, I can see how many spam messages I have. Gmail automatically deletes spam messages over thirty days old, so this is an accurate representation of how many spam messages I get in a given thirty-day period.When I first started using Gmail, my spam folder grew quickly to contain over five hundred spam messages!
